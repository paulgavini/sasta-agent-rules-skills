# Skill: Relief Lesson

## Purpose

This skill allows the agent to create relief lessons that are clear, self-contained, safe, realistic and usable by a teacher who may not know the class or subject in detail.

## When To Use

Use this skill when the user asks for:

- a relief lesson
- a cover lesson
- a lesson for a substitute teacher
- independent work for an absent teacher day
- a low-preparation lesson
- printable or LMS-ready relief instructions
- teacher and student versions of the same relief task

## Inputs

- year level, subject and topic
- lesson length and number of students
- room, equipment, software or internet availability
- what students have already learnt
- whether practical work, devices or group work are allowed
- behaviour, seating, collection or supervision notes supplied by the user
- required output or evidence for the regular teacher
- platform or format for delivery

## Process

1. Choose a task that can run safely with limited specialist knowledge.
2. Keep practical, chemical, electrical, online or high-risk activities out unless the user explicitly confirms supervision, approval and resources.
3. Make the student instructions self-contained and easy to start.
4. Make the relief teacher instructions action-first: setup, timing, supervision, collection and what to do if students finish early.
5. Include a clear required output so the regular teacher can see what was completed.
6. Add support and extension that do not require complex teacher intervention.
7. Avoid relying on logins, links, specialised software or equipment unless supplied and reliable.
8. Include behaviour, safety, privacy or platform cautions only where relevant.
9. Format the output for copying into a relief note, LMS post or printable handout.
10. State assumptions and local checks, especially for safety, devices, room access and school procedures.

## Output

```markdown
# Relief Lesson

## Relief Teacher Instructions

## Student Instructions

## Timing

## What Students Submit

## Support

## Extension

## Notes For The Regular Teacher

## Assumptions And Local Checks
```

Adjust headings to match the user's requested template or platform.

## Quality Criteria

- The relief teacher can run the lesson without extra explanation.
- Students can begin independently.
- The task is safe and realistic for cover conditions.
- Required work and submission evidence are clear.
- Support and extension are simple to manage.
- The lesson does not depend on fragile technology unless confirmed.
- Assumptions and local procedures are visible.

## Example User Requests

- `Create a Year 8 Science relief lesson on variables.`
- `Write a cover lesson for beginner Python without computers.`
- `Make a 50-minute relief task for students to complete in Daymap.`
- `Turn this worksheet into a relief lesson with teacher notes.`

## Failure Modes

- Designing a lesson that requires specialist supervision.
- Omitting what the relief teacher should collect.
- Depending on unconfirmed links, logins or software.
- Writing student instructions that require teacher explanation.
- Including practical work without safety and local approval checks.
- Making the task too long or too complex for a relief setting.

## Completion Checklist

- Can a non-specialist relief teacher run it?
- Can students start without the regular teacher?
- Is the required evidence clear?
- Are timing and early-finisher options included?
- Are safety, device and local-procedure assumptions stated?
- Is the output ready for the requested platform?
