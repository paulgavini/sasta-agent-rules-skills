# Rule: Units In Equations

## Purpose

Ensure equations, substitutions and worked examples use units accurately and visibly, especially in science and measurement contexts.

## Applies To

- physics and chemistry calculations
- science practicals and data analysis
- worked examples and answer keys
- formulas in worksheets and assessments
- graphing and measurement tasks
- Word-ready equation formatting

## Rule Statement

The agent must include appropriate units in formulas, substitutions, answers and explanations, and must not hide unit reasoning when units affect meaning or marks.

## Requirements

- Use SI units and Australian measurement conventions unless the user supplies another standard.
- Keep unit symbols accurate, including capitalisation.
- Include units in substituted values where useful.
- Include final answer units unless the quantity is dimensionless.
- Distinguish variable symbols from unit symbols.
- Keep a space between the number and unit in ordinary text, such as `5 m`.
- Use compound units clearly, such as `m/s`, `m s^-1` or the user's preferred convention.
- Check unit conversions before finalising.
- Avoid inventing units or using informal abbreviations in assessment material.
- Explain unit meaning when students are likely to confuse the quantity.

## Decision Logic

If a calculation involves measurement, include units in the setup and final answer. If the task is teaching dimensional reasoning, show unit handling explicitly. If the user requests a concise answer key, keep units correct but avoid unnecessary explanation.

## Examples

- `v = d / t = 20 m / 4 s = 5 m/s`
- `mass = 0.250 kg`
- `temperature change = 12 deg C`
- `The gradient unit is m/s because distance is in metres and time is in seconds.`

## Non-Examples

- Final answer: `5` when the answer is `5 m/s`.
- Using `M` for metres.
- Writing `10ms` when `10 ms` or `10 m s^-1` is intended.
- Cancelling units incorrectly.
- Mixing `g` and `kg` without conversion.

## Quality Check

- Are units present where needed?
- Are unit symbols accurate?
- Are conversions correct?
- Are variables and units distinct?
- Is the final answer labelled with the correct unit?
- Is the level of unit explanation suitable for students?
