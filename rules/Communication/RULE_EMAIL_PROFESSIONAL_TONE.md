# Rule: Email Professional Tone

## Purpose

Ensure school emails are clear, respectful, purposeful and appropriate for the recipient, whether written to students, families, staff, leaders or external contacts.

## Applies To

- parent and carer emails
- staff emails
- student emails
- leadership updates and requests
- follow-up messages after incidents, assessments or meetings
- LMS messages that function like email

## Rule Statement

The agent must write professional school emails that state the purpose early, use the right level of formality, protect privacy and make the requested action clear.

## Requirements

- Use a clear subject line when drafting a full email.
- Put the main purpose or required action near the start.
- Use a respectful greeting and sign-off suited to the recipient.
- Keep paragraphs short and scannable.
- Use plain language and Australian spelling.
- Match formality to the relationship, topic and school context.
- Avoid emotional, sarcastic, blaming or defensive wording.
- Keep student privacy and staff confidentiality in mind.
- Include dates, deadlines, attachments or next steps where relevant.
- Avoid overexplaining internal process unless the recipient needs it.

## Decision Logic

If the email is routine, keep it brief and action-focused. If the topic is sensitive, use a calm, factual tone and avoid judgemental language. If the message involves student wellbeing, behaviour, assessment or complaints, suggest teacher or leadership review where appropriate.

## Examples

- A parent email opens with the reason for contact and the next step.
- A staff email uses bullet points for actions and dates.
- A student email gives one clear instruction and where to find help.
- A sensitive follow-up avoids blame and invites discussion through the proper channel.

## Non-Examples

- A vague subject line such as `Hi` or `Important`.
- Long background before the actual request.
- Comments about a student's character or family.
- Casual humour in a sensitive issue email.
- Sharing identifiable student details with unnecessary recipients.

## Quality Check

- Is the subject or opening purpose clear?
- Is the tone professional and respectful?
- Is the action or next step easy to find?
- Is the level of detail appropriate?
- Is privacy protected?
- Is Australian spelling used?
