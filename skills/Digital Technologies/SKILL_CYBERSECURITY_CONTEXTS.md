# Skill: Cybersecurity Contexts

## Purpose

This skill allows the agent to create cybersecurity learning activities that are ethical, age-appropriate, defensive and grounded in Australian safety guidance.

## When To Use

Use this skill when the user asks for:

- cybersecurity lessons
- password or passphrase activities
- phishing, scams or social engineering contexts
- data protection and privacy tasks
- cyber safety scenarios
- classroom-safe threat modelling
- cybersecurity assessment prompts

## Inputs

- year level and maturity of students
- cybersecurity topic
- whether the task is student-facing or teacher-facing
- systems or accounts involved
- school policy constraints
- whether practical activity, discussion or case study is needed
- privacy and wellbeing considerations

## Process

1. Frame the activity as defensive, ethical and legal.
2. Identify the asset being protected, such as account, device, data or identity.
3. Describe the threat in age-appropriate language.
4. Focus on protective actions such as passphrases, MFA, updates, backups, privacy settings and reporting.
5. Avoid instructions that enable unauthorised access, exploitation or evasion.
6. Use fictional or public-safe scenarios.
7. Include what students should do if they see a real issue.
8. Connect cybersecurity to data ethics and privacy.
9. Check local school policy before using real accounts or incidents.

## Output

```markdown
# Cybersecurity Activity

## Scenario
## Asset To Protect
## Risk
## Safe Actions
## Discussion Questions
## Teacher Notes
## Safety Boundaries
```

## Quality Criteria

- The activity is defensive and lawful.
- Advice is practical and age-appropriate.
- No harmful operational detail is provided.
- Privacy and wellbeing risks are considered.
- Students know how to seek help or report concerns.
- Australian terminology and guidance are used.
