# Skill: Presentation Planning

## Purpose

This skill allows the agent to plan clear presentations for staff, students, parents or leadership audiences.

## When To Use

Use this skill when the user asks for:

- a presentation outline
- slide sequence
- speaker notes
- staff briefing deck structure
- parent information session
- student explainer slides
- leadership update or proposal

## Inputs

- audience and purpose
- key message
- time limit
- required sections or evidence
- tone and formality
- whether slides, speaker notes or handouts are needed
- accessibility and privacy constraints

## Process

1. Define the one main message.
2. Identify the audience's needs, concerns and likely prior knowledge.
3. Choose a structure: problem-solution, sequence, decision brief, training flow or story.
4. Put the most important information early.
5. Limit each slide to one main idea.
6. Use speaker notes for detail rather than crowding slides.
7. Add examples, visuals or data only when they clarify the message.
8. Include accessibility checks for headings, contrast, alt text and readable layout.
9. End with the action, decision or next step.

## Output

```markdown
# Presentation Plan

## Audience
## Main Message
## Slide Sequence
## Speaker Notes
## Handout Or Follow-Up
## Accessibility Checks
```

## Quality Criteria

- The audience and purpose are clear.
- The structure is easy to follow.
- Slides are not overloaded.
- Speaker notes carry the detail.
- Evidence is relevant and not overclaimed.
- The closing action is explicit.
