# Rule: SACE Alignment

## Purpose

Ensure SACE Stage 1 and Stage 2 resources, assessments, rubrics and feedback are aligned to current SACE requirements, subject outlines, learning and assessment plans, assessment design criteria and performance standards.

## Applies To

- SACE Stage 1 and Stage 2 assessment tasks
- school assessment, external assessment preparation and moderation materials
- rubrics, marking guides, feedback and grade explanations
- learning and assessment plan support
- SACE Physics, Chemistry and other subject-specific resources
- any output that claims SACE alignment or refers to SACE grades

## Rule Statement

The agent must not claim SACE alignment unless the relevant subject, stage, assessment type and standards basis are supplied by the user or checked against current SACE Board information.

## Requirements

- Confirm the SACE subject, stage, credits where relevant, assessment type and intended use.
- Use the current subject outline as the primary authority for subject content, assessment types, weightings, assessment design criteria and performance standards.
- Use the school's approved learning and assessment plan when the task depends on local assessment arrangements.
- Distinguish Stage 1 and Stage 2 requirements.
- Recognise that Stage 1 subjects are school assessed, while Stage 2 subjects commonly include school assessment and external assessment components.
- Use performance standards to describe quality of learning and evidence for grades.
- Design tasks so students have fair opportunities to demonstrate the relevant assessment design criteria and specific features.
- Preserve academic integrity by requiring assessment evidence to come from the student's own work and by noting supervision or verification needs where relevant.
- Do not invent SACE codes, assessment weightings, subject outline wording, grade boundaries or moderation requirements.
- Flag any SACE alignment as provisional if the current subject outline or school LAP has not been supplied or verified.

## Decision Logic

If the user supplies the subject outline, LAP, task sheet or performance standards, use that text first. If the user asks for a new SACE assessment and does not supply current SACE material, research the official SACE source before drafting. If subject-specific SACE material and general SACE policy both apply, use the current supplied or official subject outline as the primary authority for subject content, assessment types, weightings, criteria and performance standards. Use general SACE policy sources for cross-subject processes such as LAPs, school assessment, integrity, moderation and verification. If the request is a general teaching activity rather than an assessment, keep SACE references light and avoid implying assessment validity unless the task is mapped to standards and evidence.

## Examples

- A Stage 2 Physics task should identify the relevant assessment type and performance standards before drafting the student instructions.
- A Stage 1 Chemistry task should not assume Stage 2 external assessment weightings.
- A marking guide should refer to evidence in the student's work and the relevant performance standards, not unsupported numerical marks alone.
- A LAP support output should note where a school-approved LAP is needed before final use.

## Non-Examples

- Stating that a task is SACE-ready without checking the current subject outline or supplied LAP.
- Inventing assessment design criteria from the topic name.
- Applying Stage 2 assessment weightings to Stage 1.
- Converting SACE grades to percentages unless an official or school-approved process is supplied.
- Treating performance standards as generic rubric language disconnected from task evidence.

## Quality Check

- Has the SACE subject and stage been confirmed?
- Has the current subject outline or supplied SACE material been used?
- Is the assessment type and weighting accurate or clearly marked as needing verification?
- Are performance standards connected to evidence students will produce?
- Does the output respect the school's LAP and teacher judgement?
- Are academic integrity, moderation or verification needs noted where relevant?
