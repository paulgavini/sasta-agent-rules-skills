# Rule: Accuracy Check

## Purpose

Ensure generated content is factually, technically, mathematically, scientifically, curriculum and policy accurate before it is presented as usable teaching material.

## Applies To

- explanations, worked examples and answer keys
- curriculum and SACE alignment
- science, mathematics, digital technologies and software instructions
- safety, privacy, AI and policy guidance
- assessment tasks, marking guides and feedback
- source summaries and research-based recommendations

## Rule Statement

The agent must check claims that matter for teaching, assessment, safety, privacy or technical use, and must flag uncertainty rather than presenting unchecked claims as facts.

## Requirements

- Verify current or high-stakes claims with reputable sources.
- Check calculations, units, equations, code, terminology and examples.
- Check that curriculum, SACE and policy claims match the relevant official source.
- Distinguish established facts from interpretation, recommendation or assumption.
- Avoid invented references, statistics, file names, standards, codes or policies.
- Do not present AI-generated material as a source of truth.
- Mark uncertain content as needing verification when reliable checking is not possible.
- Prefer concise corrections over long defensive explanations.
- Re-check answer keys and marking guides against the student task.

## Decision Logic

If an error would mislead students, affect grades, create safety risk, breach privacy or waste teacher time, check it before finalising. If the claim is low-risk and stable, a light check may be enough. If the information is current, local or platform-specific, research or use supplied source material.

## Examples

- Check a balanced chemical equation and states before placing it in an answer key.
- Verify a SACE assessment requirement against SACE sources or supplied school material.
- Test or review code examples for syntax and beginner suitability.
- State that a source was not verified if web access or official material is unavailable.

## Non-Examples

- Guessing a curriculum code from memory.
- Giving a numerical answer without checking units.
- Claiming a file exists without inspecting it.
- Treating an AI response as a citation.
- Hiding uncertainty in assessment or safety advice.

## Quality Check

- Are important claims verified?
- Are calculations, units, code and terminology correct?
- Are curriculum or policy claims sourced?
- Are assumptions and uncertainty visible where needed?
- Are answer keys consistent with student tasks?
- Has unsupported material been removed?
