# Rule: No Unfair Penalty

## Purpose

Prevent assessment tasks, rubrics and marking advice from penalising students for factors that are outside the assessed criteria, unclear instructions or unsupported assumptions.

## Applies To

- assessment task design
- rubric and marking guide creation
- student work marking
- feedback, reports and grade explanations
- accommodations, scaffolding and assessment conditions

## Rule Statement

The agent must ensure penalties are tied to explicit criteria, task requirements and evidence, and must not introduce hidden expectations.

## Requirements

- Penalise only what the task and criteria make assessable.
- Do not penalise students for missing unstated requirements.
- Do not double-penalise the same error across unrelated criteria.
- Do not reduce judgement for handwriting, formatting, grammar or presentation unless they are explicit criteria or affect evidence.
- Do not penalise reasonable alternative methods if they meet the criteria.
- Distinguish content accuracy from communication quality when both are assessed.
- Account for approved accommodations and access needs where supplied.
- Keep academic integrity concerns separate from ordinary marking until verified through school processes.
- Phrase feedback as evidence-based next steps rather than blame.

## Decision Logic

If a weakness is not linked to a criterion or required evidence, do not use it to lower the judgement. If presentation affects readability enough to obscure evidence, state that the evidence cannot be clearly assessed. If integrity or authenticity is uncertain, flag it for teacher verification rather than assigning a punitive grade.

## Examples

- A science conclusion should not be penalised for decorative slide design unless presentation is assessed.
- A correct alternative algorithm should receive credit if the criteria assess problem solving rather than a specific taught method.
- If the task did not require references, missing references should be feedback for improvement rather than a hidden penalty.
- If grammar obscures meaning, assess the evidence that can be read and note the limitation.

## Non-Examples

- Deducting marks for not using a template that was not supplied.
- Penalising the same missing graph label under three criteria.
- Treating suspected AI use as proven without verification.
- Lowering a science grade because the handwriting looks rushed.
- Penalising a student for not including content beyond the taught year level.

## Quality Check

- Is every penalty linked to explicit criteria?
- Are hidden expectations avoided?
- Is double-penalising avoided?
- Are access needs and accommodations respected where supplied?
- Are integrity concerns treated through proper verification?
- Is feedback about evidence and next steps?
