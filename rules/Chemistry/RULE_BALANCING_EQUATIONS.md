# Rule: Balancing Equations

## Purpose

Ensure chemical equations are balanced accurately and explained in a way that preserves chemical meaning.

## Applies To

- chemical equation tutorials
- chemistry worked examples
- answer keys and marking guides
- practical investigation write-ups
- SACE Chemistry support materials
- Word-ready chemistry documents

## Rule Statement

The agent must balance chemical equations by changing coefficients only, not formulas, subscripts or charges.

## Requirements

- Preserve the correct chemical formula for each species.
- Balance atoms of each element on both sides of the equation.
- Change coefficients, not subscripts.
- Use the lowest whole-number coefficients unless the task requires otherwise.
- Preserve charge balance for ionic and redox equations.
- Include state symbols only when known, supplied or relevant.
- Do not invent products, states, reaction conditions or precipitates without a source or supplied context.
- For student-facing explanations, show the atom count or reasoning when useful.
- For redox, ionic or equilibrium equations, use an appropriate method rather than inspection if inspection is unreliable.
- Check the final equation against conservation of atoms and charge.

## Decision Logic

Use simple inspection for introductory equations. Use half-equation, oxidation-state or ionic methods only when the task calls for them or the reaction requires them. If formulas or products are uncertain, ask for context or flag the uncertainty before balancing.

## Examples

- `2H2 + O2 -> 2H2O`
- `CH4 + 2O2 -> CO2 + 2H2O`
- `CaCO3(s) + 2HCl(aq) -> CaCl2(aq) + CO2(g) + H2O(l)`

## Non-Examples

- Changing `H2O` to `H2O2` to balance oxygen.
- Balancing atoms but leaving net charge unbalanced in an ionic equation.
- Adding state symbols from memory when the reaction context is not supplied.
- Treating a catalyst or heat as a reactant to balance.

## Quality Check

- Are formulas preserved?
- Are atoms balanced?
- Is charge balanced where relevant?
- Are coefficients in the lowest whole-number ratio?
- Are states and products justified?
- Is the explanation suitable for the audience?
