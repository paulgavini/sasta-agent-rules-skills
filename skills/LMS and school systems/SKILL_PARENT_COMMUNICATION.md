# Skill: Parent Communication

## Purpose

This skill allows the agent to draft clear, respectful and useful communication for parents and carers about learning, assessment, behaviour, progress, meetings or school processes.

## When To Use

Use this skill when the user asks for:

- parent or carer emails
- learning progress updates
- assessment reminders or explanations
- meeting invitations or follow-ups
- behaviour or wellbeing communication
- parent-facing summaries of feedback or reports
- Daymap, Frog, Stile or LMS messages visible to families

## Inputs

- purpose of the communication
- audience and relationship to the student
- student year level and subject
- evidence or facts to include
- required action or next step
- tone, length and platform
- whether names, pronouns or placeholders should be used
- privacy, sensitivity or leadership review needs

## Process

1. Identify the purpose and desired outcome.
2. Separate facts, teacher judgement, assumptions and requested action.
3. Use parent-friendly plain language.
4. Put the main reason for contact near the start.
5. Include specific learning evidence or concern only where relevant.
6. State the next step, deadline or invitation clearly.
7. Use a calm, respectful and partnership-focused tone.
8. Remove unnecessary teacher jargon, acronyms and internal process details.
9. Check privacy and avoid mentioning other students or unnecessary personal details.
10. Flag sensitive messages for teacher or leader review before sending.

## Output

```markdown
Subject:

Dear [parent/carer name],

[Purpose and key information]

[Evidence or context]

[Next step or action]

Kind regards,
[Name]
```

For short LMS messages, omit the full email structure and use a concise announcement format.

## Quality Criteria

- Purpose is clear.
- Tone is respectful and calm.
- Families can understand what happened, what it means and what to do next.
- Sensitive information is minimised.
- The message supports partnership rather than blame.
- Australian spelling and school terminology are used.

## Example User Requests

- `Write an email to parents about an upcoming Year 8 assessment.`
- `Rewrite this parent message to be calmer and clearer.`
- `Create a Daymap post for families about missing work.`
- `Write a follow-up email after a parent meeting.`

## Failure Modes

- Blaming the student or family.
- Including unnecessary private information.
- Using unexplained school jargon.
- Making claims that are not supplied by the teacher.
- Hiding the action or next step.

## Completion Checklist

- Is the recipient and purpose clear?
- Is the main message near the start?
- Are facts and next steps accurate?
- Is the tone respectful?
- Is privacy protected?
- Should the message be reviewed before sending?
