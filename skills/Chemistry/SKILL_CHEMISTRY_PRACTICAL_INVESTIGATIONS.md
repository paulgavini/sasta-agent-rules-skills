# Skill: Chemistry Practical Investigations

## Purpose

This skill allows the agent to design or review chemistry practical investigations with clear variables, safety, method, data, analysis and assessment evidence.

## When To Use

Use this skill when the user asks for:

- a chemistry practical investigation
- a practical worksheet or report scaffold
- reaction rates practicals
- stoichiometry, acids and bases, redox or equilibrium investigations
- SACE Chemistry investigations folio support
- teacher notes for chemistry practical work

## Inputs

- year level or SACE stage
- topic, reaction or investigation question
- available chemicals, equipment and room context
- lesson length and number of lessons
- safety data, risk assessment or local constraints
- required evidence or assessment type
- data, graphing or calculation requirements
- support and extension needs

## Process

1. Define the investigation question or practical purpose.
2. Identify variables, controls and measurable evidence.
3. Check chemical formulas, concentrations, quantities and units.
4. Include safety, PPE, disposal, supervision and local risk assessment notes.
5. Design a method that is realistic for school equipment and time.
6. Provide data tables with units and repeat trials where appropriate.
7. Include graphing, calculation and uncertainty prompts.
8. Add evaluation prompts for validity, reliability, accuracy and improvements.
9. Protect assessment integrity for summative investigations.
10. Separate student instructions from teacher preparation notes.

## Output

```markdown
# Chemistry Practical Investigation

## Question Or Purpose

## Variables

## Chemicals And Equipment

## Safety And Disposal

## Method

## Data Table

## Analysis

## Evaluation

## Teacher Notes
```

Adjust sections to the user's task, school template and assessment conditions.

## Quality Criteria

- The investigation question is measurable.
- Chemistry notation and units are correct.
- Safety and disposal are visible.
- The method is realistic and controlled.
- Data collection supports analysis.
- Evaluation prompts match the evidence.
- Assessment support does not compromise integrity.

## Failure Modes

- Omitting risk assessment or disposal notes.
- Using unsafe quantities or unverified chemicals.
- Changing more than one variable at once.
- Providing data tables without units.
- Asking for analysis that the data cannot support.
- Over-scaffolding assessed investigation conclusions.

## Completion Checklist

- Is the question measurable?
- Are variables and controls clear?
- Are safety and disposal included?
- Are units and data tables correct?
- Is analysis supported by the method?
- Are local checks flagged?
