# Skill: Physics Practical Investigations

## Purpose

This skill allows the agent to design or review physics practical investigations with clear variables, measurement, safety, data quality, graphing and assessment evidence.

## When To Use

Use this skill when the user asks for:

- a physics practical investigation
- a practical worksheet or report scaffold
- a motion, force, energy, electricity or waves investigation
- a SACE Physics investigations folio task
- a graphing and uncertainty practical
- teacher notes for physics equipment or risk checks

## Inputs

- year level or SACE stage
- topic, phenomenon or question
- available equipment and room context
- lesson length and number of lessons
- required assessment evidence
- safety or local risk assessment constraints
- data collection and graphing expectations
- student prior knowledge and support needs

## Process

1. Define the investigation question or purpose.
2. Identify independent, dependent and controlled variables where appropriate.
3. Specify measurable quantities and units.
4. Choose equipment and a method that are realistic for the classroom.
5. Include safety, supervision and local risk assessment notes.
6. Provide a data table with units and repeated-trial space where useful.
7. Include graphing expectations and how students should interpret slope, trend or uncertainty.
8. Add prompts for validity, reliability, precision, limitations and improvements.
9. Protect assessment integrity by avoiding over-scaffolded final responses in summative tasks.
10. Add teacher notes for setup, troubleshooting and evidence collection.

## Output

```markdown
# Physics Practical Investigation

## Question Or Purpose

## Variables

## Equipment

## Safety And Risk Notes

## Method

## Data Table

## Graphing And Analysis

## Evaluation

## Teacher Notes
```

Adjust sections to suit the user's requested format and assessment conditions.

## Quality Criteria

- The investigation question is measurable.
- Variables and units are clear.
- Equipment and timing are realistic.
- Safety and local checks are visible.
- Data collection supports analysis.
- Graphing and uncertainty expectations are appropriate.
- Assessment evidence is collectable and fair.

- `Create a practical investigation on acceleration down a ramp.`
- `Write a SACE Physics investigations folio scaffold.`
- `Review this pendulum practical for data quality.`
- `Make a teacher-ready electricity practical with safety notes.`

## Failure Modes

- Designing a practical without measurable variables.
- Omitting units from data tables.
- Ignoring equipment or supervision constraints.
- Asking students to graph data that will not show the intended relationship.
- Treating a risky activity as safe without local checks.
- Providing too much assistance for a summative assessment.

## Completion Checklist

- Is the investigation measurable?
- Are variables, units and data tables clear?
- Are safety and local checks included?
- Is the method realistic?
- Is graph analysis planned?
- Is assessment integrity protected?
