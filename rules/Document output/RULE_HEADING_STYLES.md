# Rule: Heading Styles

## Purpose

Ensure headings make documents easier to navigate, scan, convert and use with accessibility tools.

## Applies To

- Word-ready output
- DOCX production
- Markdown intended for export
- worksheets, lesson plans and task sheets
- reports and source registers
- assessment documents and rubrics

## Rule Statement

The agent must use headings as structural signposts, not just visual decoration, and must keep heading levels logical and concise.

## Requirements

- Use one clear document title.
- Use heading levels in order: do not jump from `#` to `###` without a reason.
- Use headings for document structure rather than manually bolded lines.
- Keep headings short, descriptive and useful for scanning.
- Use sentence case unless a project template or exact title requires otherwise.
- Avoid overusing heading levels; prefer one or two levels for simple resources.
- Do not hide important headings in headers, footers, tables or text boxes when the content needs to export.
- Keep repeated section headings consistent across related resources.
- Make headings meaningful without relying on numbering alone.
- Avoid decorative emojis, symbols or punctuation in headings unless explicitly requested.

## Decision Logic

Use more heading structure when the document is long, export-bound, or teacher-facing. Use fewer headings for short student worksheets. If a Word template is supplied, preserve its heading hierarchy.

## Examples

- `# Year 8 Science: Fair Testing`
- `## Learning Goal`
- `## Task`
- `## What To Submit`
- `## Teacher Notes`

## Non-Examples

- `### Main title` as the first heading.
- `IMPORTANT BIT` as bold body text instead of a heading.
- `Section 1`, `Section 2`, `Section 3` with no descriptive wording.
- Three heading levels in a one-page worksheet.
- Hiding the document title in a table cell.

## Quality Check

- Is there one clear title?
- Do heading levels follow a logical order?
- Are headings descriptive and concise?
- Will headings work if converted to Word or PDF?
- Are fake headings avoided?
- Is the structure appropriate for the document length?
