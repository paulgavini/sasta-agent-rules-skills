# Rule: Year Level Appropriateness

## Purpose

Ensure resources, explanations, examples, questions and assessments are pitched at the correct year level or SACE stage, with suitable cognitive demand, vocabulary, scaffolding and independence.

## Applies To

- student-facing worksheets, tutorials and lesson materials
- teacher notes, lesson sequences and relief lessons
- assessment tasks, rubrics and feedback
- worked examples, explanations and extension activities
- Australian Curriculum F-10 and SACE Stage 1 or Stage 2 materials

## Rule Statement

The agent must match the depth, language, abstraction and expected independence of the output to the user's stated year level, SACE stage, subject and classroom context.

## Requirements

- Use the year level, SACE stage or supplied student profile before making assumptions.
- Check relevant curriculum, achievement standards or SACE performance standards when the pitch affects assessment validity.
- Match vocabulary to the students' likely prior learning, introducing technical terms only when needed.
- Build explanations from familiar ideas to new concepts.
- Use examples, data, contexts and quantities that suit the age group and subject.
- Provide scaffolding for novice learners and remove unnecessary scaffolding for advanced or senior learners.
- Keep cognitive load manageable by avoiding too many new ideas, representations or instructions at once.
- Avoid adult professional, university or industry-level expectations unless the user asks for extension.
- Flag uncertainty when the year level, class ability range or prior learning is unclear.

## Decision Logic

If the user supplies a year level, that year level controls the pitch unless they also ask for extension or support. If no year level is supplied and the task is low-risk, infer cautiously from the topic and state the assumption. If the task is an assessment, SACE item, safety activity or high-stakes resource, ask or research before drafting.

## Boundary With Context And Overteaching Rules

Use `rules/Core behaviour/RULE_FOLLOW_USER_CONTEXT.md` to identify the user's stated year level, stage, platform and audience. Use this rule to set the pitch, vocabulary, abstraction and expected independence. Use `rules/Curriculum/RULE_NO_OVERTEACHING.md` to remove unnecessary extra content after the pitch is set.

## Examples

- A Year 8 resource should use short, explicit instructions and concrete examples before abstract terminology.
- A Stage 2 Physics worked example can expect algebraic manipulation, but should still show reasoning where it supports learning.
- A mixed-ability class may need a core task plus optional extension rather than one task pitched above the whole class.
- A rubric should assess the intended year-level evidence, not senior-level polish unless that is part of the standard.

## Non-Examples

- Giving Year 7 students an unscaffolded senior secondary investigation structure.
- Adding university-level theory because it is technically related.
- Using complex terminology without teaching or defining it.
- Assuming all students have mastered prerequisite skills that the task depends on.
- Treating extension material as the expected standard for all students.

## Quality Check

- Is the year level or SACE stage clear?
- Does the task match the relevant curriculum or standards evidence?
- Are vocabulary, examples and instructions suitable for the students?
- Is scaffolding proportional to the expected independence?
- Are extension and support clearly separated from core expectations?
- Have assumptions about prior learning been stated where needed?
