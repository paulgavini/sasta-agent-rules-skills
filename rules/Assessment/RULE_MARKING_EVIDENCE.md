# Rule: Marking Evidence

## Purpose

Ensure marking, grading and feedback are based on observable evidence in student work rather than assumptions, effort, behaviour, personality or unsupported inference.

## Applies To

- marking guides, rubrics and answer keys
- student work marking and grade explanations
- SACE performance standards and Australian Curriculum achievement standards
- report comments and feedback
- moderation and evidence review

## Rule Statement

The agent must ground every assessment judgement in evidence from the submitted work and the relevant criteria or standards.

## Requirements

- Identify the criteria, rubric, achievement standard or performance standard being applied.
- Refer to specific evidence in the student's work when making a judgement.
- Distinguish what the student demonstrated from what they may have intended.
- Do not reward or penalise effort, behaviour, neatness or compliance unless those are explicit criteria.
- Do not infer understanding from unsupported claims.
- Use missing evidence as missing evidence, not as proof of incapacity.
- Avoid making personal comments about the student.
- Keep grade explanations tied to task requirements and standards.
- Flag cases where evidence is insufficient, ambiguous or possibly not the student's own work.
- Preserve privacy by anonymising examples where possible.

## Decision Logic

If the evidence is present, judge it against the criteria. If evidence is absent, say what is missing and what cannot be judged. If the evidence is unclear, identify the uncertainty rather than guessing. If assessment integrity is uncertain, flag that teacher or school verification is needed.

## Examples

- "The response identifies the independent variable and gives a controlled variable, but does not explain how the control improves validity."
- "The code runs for the sample input but does not handle invalid input, which limits evidence for robustness."
- "No conclusion is submitted, so the criterion about interpreting results cannot be assessed."
- "This judgement is provisional because the supplied extract does not include the full task."

## Non-Examples

- "This student tried hard, so they should receive a B."
- "They probably understand it but ran out of time."
- "The work is messy, so the science is weak."
- "This sounds like an A response" without linking to criteria.
- Penalising missing formatting when formatting is not assessed.

## Quality Check

- Is each judgement linked to evidence?
- Is the relevant criterion or standard identified?
- Are assumptions avoided?
- Are missing or ambiguous evidence handled fairly?
- Is feedback about the work, not the person?
- Are privacy and integrity concerns flagged?
