# Rule: Staff Facing Language

## Purpose

Ensure communication to colleagues and school staff is clear, respectful, efficient and useful for shared teaching, administration and student-support work.

## Applies To

- staff emails and announcements
- meeting notes and agendas
- teacher instructions and handover notes
- relief teacher notes
- leadership updates
- shared planning and assessment communication

## Rule Statement

The agent must write staff-facing communication that respects professional time, states actions clearly and handles student or staff information appropriately.

## Requirements

- Put the purpose, decision or action near the start.
- Use clear headings or bullet points for multiple items.
- Include dates, owners, deadlines and links where relevant.
- Use a collegial, respectful and practical tone.
- Keep internal acronyms only where the audience will understand them.
- Avoid blame, sarcasm or unnecessary emotional wording.
- Protect student, family and staff privacy.
- Distinguish confirmed information from assumptions or pending decisions.
- Make requests specific and achievable.
- Keep the level of detail proportional to the staff need.

## Decision Logic

If the communication asks staff to act, make the action visible. If it is an update, highlight what changed and who needs to know. If student information is included, minimise detail to what staff need for their role.

## Examples

- A staff update starts with "Action required by Friday" and lists the steps.
- A relief note gives class, room, timing, behaviour notes and work to collect.
- A moderation email names the task, due date and evidence to bring.
- A meeting agenda separates discussion items from decisions needed.

## Non-Examples

- Long narrative emails where the action is hidden.
- Naming students unnecessarily in a broad staff message.
- Using a frustrated tone about colleagues or classes.
- Assuming all staff understand niche subject acronyms.
- Sending unresolved speculation as fact.

## Quality Check

- Is the purpose or action clear?
- Are dates, owners and next steps included where needed?
- Is the tone collegial and professional?
- Is sensitive information minimised?
- Is the message easy to scan?
- Are assumptions labelled?
