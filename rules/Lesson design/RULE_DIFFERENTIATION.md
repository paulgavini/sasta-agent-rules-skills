# Rule: Differentiation

## Purpose

Ensure lesson materials include appropriate support and extension for diverse learners without lowering expectations, overcomplicating the task or making assumptions about students.

## Applies To

- lesson plans and sequences
- worksheets and tutorials
- assessment preparation
- relief lessons
- practical investigations
- digital technologies and science tasks
- teacher notes and implementation advice

## Rule Statement

The agent must design differentiation that supports access, challenge and participation while keeping the core learning goal clear.

## Requirements

- Identify the core learning expectation first.
- Provide support options that help students access the same learning goal where possible.
- Provide extension that deepens or transfers learning, not just more work.
- Avoid deficit labels for students.
- Use inclusive language and respect diverse learning, language, cultural and disability needs.
- Distinguish teacher-facing differentiation notes from student-facing instructions.
- Keep adjustments practical for a classroom teacher.
- Avoid inventing individual needs or accommodations not supplied by the user.
- Flag when formal adjustments or disability supports require teacher or school processes.

## Decision Logic

If the user gives student needs, tailor supports to those needs. If the user gives no class profile, include general support and extension options. If the task is assessment, ensure support does not compromise the evidence being assessed.

## Examples

- Support: provide a partially completed data table for students who need help organising observations.
- Extension: ask students to evaluate how changing a controlled variable could affect validity.
- Language support: define key vocabulary before the task.
- Teacher note: offer a choice of pair work or independent work depending on class needs.

## Non-Examples

- Giving struggling students unrelated easier work.
- Calling students `low ability` in student-facing materials.
- Adding extension as simply extra questions of the same type.
- Providing unauthorised assessment assistance.
- Assuming disability, language background or behaviour needs without context.

## Quality Check

- Is the core learning expectation clear?
- Do supports preserve access to the learning goal?
- Does extension deepen learning?
- Is the language respectful and inclusive?
- Are teacher-facing notes separate where needed?
- Are formal adjustment needs flagged?
