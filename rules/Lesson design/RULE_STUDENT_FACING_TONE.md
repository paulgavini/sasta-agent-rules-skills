# Rule: Student Facing Tone

## Purpose

Ensure student-facing lesson materials are clear, encouraging, age-appropriate and focused on what students need to do and learn.

## Applies To

- worksheets and tutorials
- LMS lesson posts
- assessment instructions
- practical methods
- coding tasks
- feedback and revision materials
- relief lesson instructions

## Rule Statement

The agent must write student-facing content in plain, direct and respectful language that supports independence without sounding childish or vague.

## Requirements

- Address students directly where appropriate.
- Put the task or learning action near the start.
- Use short sentences and clear steps.
- Define essential technical terms.
- Avoid teacher-only jargon and internal curriculum language.
- Keep tone calm, respectful and encouraging.
- Use age-appropriate wording for the year level.
- Do not overpraise or use gimmicky language.
- Separate extension from required work.
- Make submission or completion expectations clear.

## Decision Logic

If students need to act, use clear imperative steps. If students need to understand a concept, use plain explanation and examples. If the content is for senior students, keep the tone mature and precise. If the content is for junior students, use more scaffolding without talking down to them.

## Examples

- `Record your measurements in the table. Include units in each column heading.`
- `Your goal is to explain how changing one variable affected the result.`
- `Extension: evaluate one source of uncertainty in your data.`

## Non-Examples

- `Students will demonstrate curriculum-aligned inquiry competencies.`
- `Just have a go!!!`
- Long paragraphs before the actual instruction.
- Hidden teacher notes in the student task.
- Overly casual jokes in assessment instructions.

## Quality Check

- Is the task clear to students?
- Is the tone respectful and age-appropriate?
- Are instructions short and direct?
- Are technical terms explained?
- Is extension clearly labelled?
- Are teacher-only notes removed?
