# Skill: Electronics Circuits

## Purpose

This skill allows the agent to create beginner electronics circuit activities that are safe, readable and suitable for classroom practical work.

## When To Use

Use this skill when the user asks for:

- simple circuit lessons
- breadboard activities
- LED, resistor, switch or battery tasks
- Ohm's law worked examples
- circuit diagrams or troubleshooting
- teacher notes for electronics practicals

## Inputs

- year level and prior circuit experience
- available components and power source
- whether breadboards, micro:bits or kits are used
- target concept: current, voltage, resistance, series, parallel or switching
- required calculations or measurements
- safety constraints and supervision level
- required output format

## Process

1. Define the circuit goal and learning concept.
2. List components and power source.
3. Draw or describe the circuit before building.
4. Identify polarity-sensitive components such as LEDs.
5. Include current-limiting resistors where needed.
6. Build and test one section at a time.
7. Use Ohm's law only where it suits the level and component behaviour.
8. Troubleshoot by checking power, polarity, continuity, connections and component placement.
9. Include safety notes for batteries, short circuits, heat, exposed boards and supervision.

## Output

```markdown
# Electronics Activity

## Goal
## Components
## Circuit Plan
## Build Steps
## Test
## Troubleshooting
## Safety Notes
```

## Quality Criteria

- Components and power requirements are explicit.
- The circuit is built progressively.
- Safety and supervision are visible.
- Calculations use correct units.
- Troubleshooting is practical.
- The task stays within low-voltage classroom conditions.
