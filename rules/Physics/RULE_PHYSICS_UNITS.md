# Rule: Physics Units

## Purpose

Ensure physics resources use units consistently, accurately and in ways that support student understanding, calculation, graphing and assessment evidence.

## Applies To

- physics worked examples
- graph analysis
- practical investigations
- formulas and substitutions
- answer keys and marking guides
- SACE Physics tasks
- Word-ready equation resources

## Rule Statement

The agent must use correct SI units, unit symbols, prefixes and conversions in physics outputs, and must show units where they affect meaning, reasoning or marks.

## Requirements

- Use SI units unless the user, source material or local task specifies otherwise.
- Keep unit symbols accurate, including capitalisation.
- Include a space between a number and unit symbol, such as `12 m` or `4.5 s`.
- Do not put a space between an SI prefix and unit symbol, such as `ms`, `km` or `mA`.
- Distinguish similar-looking symbols, such as `m` for metre and `M` only where a source explicitly uses it for another meaning.
- Show units in substitutions when students are learning the method.
- Include final answer units unless the quantity is dimensionless.
- Use consistent compound-unit notation, such as `m/s`, `m s^-1`, `N m` or the user's supplied convention.
- Check unit conversions before finalising, especially `g` to `kg`, `cm` to `m`, `ms` to `s`, and `kJ` to `J`.
- For graph gradients and areas, derive units from the axis units.

## Decision Logic

If the output is student-facing, show enough unit handling for students to learn the reasoning. If it is an answer key, include concise unit checks. If it is SACE or assessment-facing, preserve the notation and unit conventions from the supplied task, formula sheet or subject outline where available.

## Examples

- `v = d / t = 20 m / 4.0 s = 5.0 m/s`
- `F = ma = 2.0 kg x 3.0 m/s^2 = 6.0 N`
- Gradient of a velocity-time graph: `(m/s) / s = m/s^2`
- Area under a force-distance graph: `N m = J`

## Non-Examples

- Writing `5` instead of `5 m/s`.
- Using `10ms` when the intended value is `10 m/s`.
- Mixing `g` and `kg` in one substitution without conversion.
- Treating graph gradient units as optional.
- Changing a supplied formula-sheet convention without reason.

## Quality Check

- Are all required units present?
- Are unit symbols and prefixes correct?
- Are conversions checked?
- Are graph gradient and area units derived correctly?
- Are student-facing unit steps clear enough?
- Is the notation consistent with the task or course context?
