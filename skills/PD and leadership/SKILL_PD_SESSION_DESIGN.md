# Skill: PD Session Design

## Purpose

This skill allows the agent to design professional development sessions that are purposeful, practical and aligned to teacher learning needs.

## When To Use

Use this skill when the user asks for:

- a staff PD session
- workshop agenda
- professional learning plan
- AI, curriculum, assessment or platform training
- facilitator notes
- participant activities and follow-up tasks

## Inputs

- session topic and purpose
- audience, role and experience level
- available time and delivery mode
- desired outcomes
- local priorities or standards links
- tools, examples or artefacts to use
- follow-up evidence or implementation task

## Process

1. Define the professional learning need.
2. Identify what participants should know, practise and take away.
3. Connect the session to school priorities or professional standards where useful.
4. Use a focused structure: context, model, practise, discuss, apply, reflect.
5. Include realistic teacher artefacts or scenarios.
6. Build in collaboration without making it performative.
7. Provide facilitator notes and timing.
8. Add follow-up implementation and evidence of impact.
9. Keep slides or handouts concise and usable after the session.

## Output

```markdown
# PD Session Plan

## Purpose
## Audience
## Outcomes
## Agenda
## Activities
## Facilitator Notes
## Resources
## Follow-Up
```

## Quality Criteria

- The session has a clear learning need.
- Activities are practical and time-realistic.
- Teachers leave with a usable artefact or next step.
- Discussion is purposeful.
- Follow-up supports implementation.
- Standards or priorities are included only when useful.
