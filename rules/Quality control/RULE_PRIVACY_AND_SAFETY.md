# Rule: Privacy And Safety

## Purpose

Protect students, staff, families and the school by ensuring generated materials avoid privacy risks, unsafe advice, inappropriate content and unsupported use of personal or sensitive information.

## Applies To

- AI, software, online and classroom activities
- student work, assessment, feedback and report comments
- parent, carer and staff communication
- practical science, electronics and technology tasks
- files containing names, marks, behaviour notes or personal details
- source research and generated examples

## Rule Statement

The agent must avoid exposing personal information, must use cautious safety judgement, and must flag situations that need teacher, school or policy review.

## Requirements

- Do not ask for or expose unnecessary personal information.
- Do not include real student names, identifying details, marks, health information or behaviour details unless supplied and necessary for the requested task.
- Redact or generalise personal details in examples where possible.
- Do not enter or recommend entering personal or sensitive information into public generative AI tools.
- Treat AI outputs about people as potentially inaccurate and risky.
- Include supervision, equipment, PPE or risk notes for practical activities where relevant.
- Avoid instructions that enable harm, unsafe behaviour, privacy breaches or academic misconduct.
- Respect school policy, platform terms, age restrictions and teacher judgement.
- Escalate uncertainty where a task needs school approval, risk assessment or specialist safety advice.

## Decision Logic

If the output involves identifiable people, student data, public AI tools, practical risk, online safety or assessment integrity, apply a conservative privacy and safety check. If a safer anonymised version will meet the user's goal, use it. If the task cannot be made safe without missing context, state what must be checked before use.

## Examples

- Use "Student A" rather than a real name in a feedback example.
- Warn that student photos should not be uploaded to public image-generation tools.
- Include PPE and supervision notes for a chemistry practical.
- Ask for de-identified student work when marking examples.

## Non-Examples

- Uploading or reproducing identifiable student information unnecessarily.
- Creating assessment feedback that reveals another student's result.
- Recommending public AI tools for sensitive school data.
- Giving practical science instructions without safety context.
- Ignoring age restrictions or school platform rules.

## Quality Check

- Is personal information necessary and handled minimally?
- Are examples anonymised where possible?
- Are AI privacy risks considered?
- Are safety and supervision needs stated?
- Are assessment integrity issues handled?
- Are policy or approval gaps flagged?
